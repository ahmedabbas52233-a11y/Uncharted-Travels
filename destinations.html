Ancient
Sindh

Sindh cradles the oldest chapter of Pakistan's story. Mohenjo-daro, built in 2500 BCE with a sewage system more advanced than contemporary Rome, sits here. So does the vast Makli Necropolis — one of the world's largest Islamic burial grounds — and the 17th-century port city of Thatta. Sindh is where human civilisation in this subcontinent began.

Frontier
Khyber

The crossroads of civilisations. KPK was the heartland of Gandhara — where Greek sculpture merged with Buddhist spirituality to create one of antiquity's most remarkable art forms. Today Swat, Peshawar, Mardan, and Chitral preserve that legacy alongside living traditions: the Kalash people, whose culture predates Islam in the region, still celebrate ancient spring festivals in their highland valleys.

04

Province

Vast
Balochistan

Pakistan's largest and least-visited province holds some of its most extraordinary secrets. Mehrgarh, near Quetta, is one of the earliest known farming settlements on Earth — older than the pyramids, older than Mesopotamia. The Bolān Pass has been the gateway through which armies and traders have entered the subcontinent for five millennia. Balochistan rewards the patient, adventurous traveller above all others.

Gilgit-

Where the Karakoram, Hindu Kush, and Himalayan mountain ranges converge, Gilgit-Baltistan holds not just the world's most dramatic scenery but some of its oldest Silk Road trading posts and Buddhist rock carvings. The ancient forts of Hunza — Baltit and Altit — overlook valleys that Persian, Chinese, and Central Asian merchants traversed for over a thousand years.

Curator's Selection

Absolute
Must-Sees

🏛️
Badshahi Mosque
Lahore · Punjab
The world's largest mosque when completed in 1673. Aurangzeb's most ambitious architectural statement — pure Mughal power made manifest in red sandstone and white marble.
🏺
Taxila Museum
Taxila · Punjab
The finest collection of Gandhara sculpture on Earth. Fasting Buddhas, stucco reliefs, and gold jewellery from an empire that fused Greek and Buddhist art 2,000 years ago.
🌊
Mohenjo-daro
Larkana · Sindh
The Great Bath. The world's first urban planning. Streets laid on a grid 2,500 years before Rome. Walking through Mohenjo-daro is the closest you can come to time travel.
🔱
Wazir Khan Mosque
Lahore · Punjab
The most elaborately decorated mosque in Pakistan. Every inch of tile work is a lesson in Mughal artistry — floral, calligraphic, geometric, all alive with colour after 400 years.
🏔️
Baltit Fort
Hunza · GB
Perched above Hunza Valley with Rakaposhi behind it. A thousand-year-old royal residence that controlled the Silk Road passes between China and the Indian subcontinent.

When to Visit

Seasons &
Best Times

🌸
Spring
March — May

Ideal for Lahore, Taxila, Peshawar and KPK. Blooming orchards in Hunza. Comfortable temperatures throughout Punjab and Sindh before the summer heat builds.

Best for: Punjab Heritage Trail, KPK Gandhara Tour

☀️
Summer
June — August

Escape the plains and head to Gilgit-Baltistan and KPK highlands. Skardu, Hunza, Swat, and Chitral are at their most spectacular. Plains sites (Lahore, Sindh) are best avoided.

Best for: Gilgit-Baltistan, KKH, Chitral

🍂
Autumn
September — November

The single best season for all-Pakistan travel. Golden light, cooler temperatures everywhere, harvest festivals in Kalash valleys. Mohenjo-daro, Makli, and Lahore are outstanding.

Best for: All regions · Our recommended season

❄️
Winter
December — February

Perfect for Sindh (Mohenjo-daro, Makli) and Balochistan (Mehrgarh, Quetta). Lahore in winter mist is magical. Northern areas are snow-closed but uniquely beautiful.

Best for: Sindh Heritage, Balochistan, Lahore
