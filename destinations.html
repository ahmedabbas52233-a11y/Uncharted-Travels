Punjab —
Heart of Empire

Pakistan's most populous province carries the weight of empires — Mauryan, Kushan, Delhi Sultanate, Mughal, and British. Punjab's soil holds the ruins of Taxila (three UNESCO listings in one site) and the walled city of Lahore, one of South Asia's greatest Mughal capitals.

Sindh —
Cradle of Civilisation

Sindh is where urban life began. Mohenjo-daro, built in 2500 BCE, was the world's first planned city — with indoor plumbing, standardised weights, and city blocks laid on a precise grid. The Makli Necropolis holds 500,000 graves across 10 sq km, one of the largest medieval cemeteries on Earth.

KP —
Gandhara's Heartland

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa is where Buddhist civilisation reached its artistic peak. The Gandhara School of Art — a fusion of Hellenistic and Indian styles born from Alexander's passage through — produced some of antiquity's finest sculpture. Takht-i-Bahi remains the best-preserved Buddhist monastery in South Asia.

Balochistan —
Where Civilisation Began

Mehrgarh — discovered in 1974 — is older than Mesopotamia and Egypt. Dating to 7000 BCE, it's the earliest evidence of farming and settled life in South Asia. Balochistan also holds the world's strangest geological landscape: the Makran Coastal Highway, mud volcanoes, and the Princess of Hope rock formation.

Gilgit-Baltistan —
Where Silk Roads Met

The world's highest concentration of 7,000m+ peaks. The meeting point of the Silk Road's three main branches. Home to the Hunza Kingdom's 700-year-old Baltit Fort, Chilas Rock Art dating to 6000 BCE, and the Karakoram Highway — the world's highest international paved road.

When to Visit

Pakistan Through
the Seasons

🍂
Autumn
October – November
The golden season. Perfect temperatures everywhere, harvest celebrations in the north, and Lahore's gardens in bloom. Best for Punjab, KPK, and GB.
Best for: All regions equally
❄️
Winter
December – February
Ideal for Sindh and Balochistan — cool, dry, and perfect for outdoor ruins. Snow blocks mountain passes but Lahore and Karachi are excellent.
Best for: Sindh · Balochistan · Punjab
🌸
Spring
March – April
Cherry blossoms in the north, wildflowers in Balochistan. Second-best season overall — comfortable everywhere before the heat arrives.
Best for: GB · KPK · Balochistan
☀️
Summer
May – September
Hot in the plains (40°C+) but magnificent in the mountains. Gilgit-Baltistan and KPK highlands are at their finest. Monsoon brings drama to Sindh.
Best for: Gilgit-Baltistan · KPK mountains
