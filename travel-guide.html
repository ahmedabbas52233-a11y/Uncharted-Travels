Entry Requirements

Visas &
Entry

e-Visa System (evisa.gov.pk)

Pakistan operates an online e-Visa system for citizens of 175+ countries. Most visitors apply entirely online — no embassy visit required. Processing typically takes 3–10 business days. The e-Visa is emailed to you and shown on arrival.

Tourist visa validity: 30 days single entry or 90 days multiple entry. Extensions are available from the Ministry of Interior in Islamabad, Lahore, or Karachi.

  • ✓Apply at evisa.gov.pk — straightforward process
  • ✓US, UK, EU, Australian citizens all eligible
  • ✓Standard fee: ~$50–75 USD depending on nationality
  • !Apply at least 2 weeks before travel to allow processing time
  • !Passport must be valid for 6 months beyond entry date
  • ✗Israeli passport holders are not admitted

Special Permits: Northern Areas

Most of Pakistan requires only a standard tourist visa. However, certain sensitive border areas require No Objection Certificates (NOC) — these are usually arranged by tour operators like us before your trip.

Areas requiring NOC: Khunjerab Pass border crossing into China, certain valleys in Chitral near the Afghan border, areas near the Line of Control in Azad Kashmir.

  • ✓We arrange all NOCs for tours in sensitive areas
  • ✓Process takes 7–14 days before travel
  • ✓Most popular tourist areas require no NOC at all
  • !Gilgit-Baltistan: foreigners must register with local police within 24h of arrival
  • !Chitral: NOC recommended for Kalash valleys and border areas
02

Arrival

Getting
to Pakistan

✈️
By Air — International Airports

Pakistan has three major international airports: Islamabad (ISB) — the most modern and best connected for northern heritage sites. Lahore (LHE) — ideal for Punjab heritage. Karachi (KHI) — gateway to Sindh and Balochistan.

Major carriers: PIA, Turkish Airlines, Emirates, Qatar Airways, Etihad. Best fares are typically via Istanbul, Dubai, or Doha.

Most Used Route
🚌
Overland from China

The Karakoram Highway (KKH) connects Pakistan to China via the Khunjerab Pass at 4,693m — the world's highest international border crossing. Open May–November (weather dependent). Cross by bus from Kashgar to Gilgit — one of the world's great road journeys.

Requires a Chinese visa and a Pakistan NOC for the border area. We can arrange both.

Adventure Route
🚂
Overland from India

The Wagah Border Crossing between Lahore and Amritsar is the only land border between India and Pakistan open to foreign nationals. The famous flag-lowering ceremony here is a spectacle not to be missed.

Requires advance arrangement — a Pakistani visa obtained before arrival at border. Relations between the two countries affect crossing availability; check current status before planning.

03

Getting Around

Transport
Inside Pakistan

🚗
Private Car + Driver

The recommended option for heritage tourism. A driver who knows the roads, the sites, and the local context is invaluable. We provide vetted drivers with all our tours. Cost: ~$60–100 per day including fuel.

✈️
Domestic Flights

PIA, Airblue, and Serene Air connect Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, Quetta, Peshawar, Gilgit, and Skardu. The Islamabad–Skardu flight offers spectacular mountain views. Book early — small aircraft fill quickly. Delays common in north due to weather.

🚆
Train

Pakistan Railways runs services between major cities. The Lahore–Karachi and Lahore–Peshawar routes are scenic and comfortable in AC Business class. A quintessential Pakistani experience — chai vendors, packed platforms, unexpected conversations. Journey times are long but enjoyable.

🚌
Bus Services

Daewoo Express, Faisal Movers, and Skyways run comfortable AC coaches between major Punjab and KPK cities. For northern areas (Gilgit, Hunza), local Coaster minibuses ply the KKH. NATCO buses serve remote northern areas. Book ahead for busy routes.

Money &

Currency & Exchange

Pakistan's currency is the Pakistani Rupee (PKR). As of 2025, approximately 280–290 PKR = 1 USD. Pakistan is a largely cash-based economy — carry sufficient rupees for rural areas.

Best exchange method: Exchange major currencies (USD, EUR, GBP) at exchange companies in large cities — rates are significantly better than banks. Avoid airport exchange counters.

  • ✓USD is the most useful foreign currency to carry
  • ✓ATMs widely available in cities (HBL, UBL, MCB reliable)
  • ✓Visa/Mastercard accepted at major hotels and restaurants
  • !Rural areas and northern Pakistan: cash only
  • !Notify your bank before travel to avoid card blocks
  • !Carry enough cash for 2–3 days in northern areas

05

Security

Safety &
What to Know

Pakistan's international reputation significantly overstates the security risks for tourists visiting its heritage sites. The country has undergone a dramatic security transformation since 2014. The vast majority of travellers — including those on solo trips — complete their journeys without incident. Millions of domestic tourists visit Pakistan's historical sites every year.

Lahore & Punjab Heritage Sites

Extremely safe for tourists. Lahore is a vibrant, well-policed city with millions of domestic and international visitors. Police Tourist Squad provides dedicated support for foreign visitors. Standard urban travel precautions apply.

Taxila & Islamabad Region

Very safe. Islamabad and Rawalpindi are Pakistan's most security-conscious cities. Taxila is a well-established tourist destination with regular visitors. Comfortable for solo travellers and families.

Gilgit-Baltistan

Generally very safe and welcoming. Thousands of international trekkers visit annually without incident. Weather and terrain risks are the primary concerns — not security. Register with local police as required.

Sindh (Karachi, Mohenjo-daro)

Karachi requires more caution than northern cities — petty crime is higher. Tourist sites themselves are generally safe. Travel with a guide, use reputable transport, avoid certain neighbourhoods after dark. Mohenjo-daro (Larkana) is remote but safe.

KPK — Peshawar, Swat

Situation has improved dramatically since 2015. Swat and Peshawar are popular with domestic tourists and increasingly with international visitors. Travel with a registered local guide. Check current FCO/US State Dept advisories for specific areas before departure.

Certain Balochistan Areas

Western Balochistan near the Afghan and Iranian borders requires caution. Quetta, Ziarat, and Mehrgarh are more accessible but should be visited with local guidance. We will advise on current conditions when planning a Balochistan itinerary.

Medical Preparation

Health &
Vaccinations

💉
Recommended Vaccinations

Consult your GP or travel clinic 6–8 weeks before departure. Generally recommended for Pakistan:

Routine: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, Tetanus-Diphtheria-Pertussis update

Consider: Hepatitis B, Rabies (if visiting rural areas or caves), Cholera (if staying long-term)

Required by law: Yellow Fever vaccination certificate if arriving from endemic countries.

See GP 6–8 Weeks Before
💊
Water & Food Safety

Never drink tap water in Pakistan. Bottled water is inexpensive and widely available everywhere. Avoid ice in drinks outside major hotels.

Food: Cooked food from busy restaurants and street stalls is generally safe — high turnover means fresh ingredients. Avoid raw salads and unpeeled fruit at cheaper establishments.

Carry oral rehydration salts and a basic antibiotic (prescribed by your doctor) for stomach issues.

Bottled Water Only
🏔️
Altitude Sickness

If visiting Gilgit-Baltistan, Chitral, or travelling the KKH, altitude sickness is a real concern. The Skardu airport (2,228m) and Khunjerab Pass (4,693m) require acclimatisation.

Ascend gradually — spend 1–2 nights in Gilgit (1,500m) before heading higher. Symptoms: headache, nausea, dizziness. Descend immediately if symptoms are severe.

Acetazolamide (Diamox) can help with acclimatisation — consult your doctor before travel.

07

Preparation

What to
Pack

🧥 Clothing

  • Loose, modest clothing — covered shoulders & knees
  • Women: lightweight headscarf for religious sites
  • Warm layers for northern areas (evenings are cold)
  • Comfortable walking shoes — uneven ground at ruins
  • Sandals for easy removal at mosques & shrines
  • Rain jacket for KPK and monsoon travel (Jul–Sep)
  • Sunglasses and wide-brim hat (sun is intense)
  • Thermal base layers for Gilgit-Baltistan winter visits

🎒 Essentials

  • Passport + printed e-Visa copy
  • Travel insurance with medical evacuation cover
  • Unlocked smartphone for local SIM card
  • Power bank (load-shedding is common)
  • Universal power adapter (Type C and D sockets)
  • Cash in USD and local rupees
  • Downloaded offline maps (Google Maps or Maps.me)
  • Photocopies of all documents kept separately

🩺 Health & Safety

  • Prescribed medications with doctor's letter
  • Oral rehydration salts (ORS)
  • Antidiarrheal medication
  • High-SPF sunscreen (50+)
  • Insect repellent (DEET-based for rural Sindh)
  • Water purification tablets as backup
  • Basic first aid kit (plasters, antiseptic, bandages)
  • Altitude sickness medication if visiting north
