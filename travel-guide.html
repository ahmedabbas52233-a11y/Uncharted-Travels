Entry Requirements

Visas &
Entry

🌐
E-Visa on Arrival

Available to citizens of 50+ countries including USA, UK, EU nations, Canada, Australia, and most Asian countries. Apply online at NADRA's portal — approved within 24–72 hours. Cost: USD 30–75 depending on nationality.

Most Travellers
✈️
Visa on Arrival

Available at Islamabad, Lahore, and Karachi airports for eligible nationalities. Bring a hotel confirmation letter and sufficient funds. Processing takes 15–30 minutes. USD 75 fee payable in cash.

Airport Only
📋
Standard Visa

For nationalities not covered by e-visa. Apply at your nearest Pakistan Embassy. Single-entry tourist visas are typically valid for 30–90 days. Processing time: 5–15 business days. Invite letter from a Pakistani contact helps.

Embassy Application
🔖
Special Area Permits

Gilgit-Baltistan requires a No Objection Certificate (NOC) for some areas near borders. Tribal Districts in KPK may require additional permits. We arrange all permits for booked tours.

Mountain Regions

Customs & Imports

Pakistan customs are straightforward for tourists. Key points to note:

  • ✓
    Camera equipment: declare expensive gear to avoid export questions
  • ✓
    Medicines: carry prescriptions for any controlled medication
  • ✗
    Alcohol: importing alcohol is prohibited
  • ✗
    Drones: require advance registration with Civil Aviation Authority
  • ✗
    Satellite phones: require special permit

Travel Safety

Safety in
Pakistan

Punjab (Lahore, Taxila, Rawalpindi)

Very safe for tourists. Lahore in particular is one of South Asia's great tourist cities — welcoming, cosmopolitan, and with excellent infrastructure. Taxila and Rawalpindi are routine tourist destinations. Normal urban awareness applies.

Sindh (Karachi, Mohenjo-daro)

Karachi has improved dramatically since 2013. The tourist circuit — Clifton, heritage buildings, Mohenjo-daro, Makli — is safe with normal precautions. Avoid travelling alone at night in unfamiliar neighbourhoods.

KPK (Peshawar, Swat, Chitral)

Major tourist areas — Swat, Peshawar, Takht-i-Bahi, Chitral — are generally safe and see significant tourist traffic. Check current UK/US/your-government travel advisories. Our guides know current conditions in real time.

Balochistan (Quetta, Gwadar)

Some parts of Balochistan require caution. Tourist routes — Mehrgarh, Ziarat, Quetta — are generally accessible. The Makran Coastal Highway is routinely driven by travellers. We always advise on current conditions before tours in this province.

Gilgit-Baltistan (Hunza, Skardu)

One of the safest and most welcoming regions in Pakistan. Hunza Valley is a well-established tourist destination with infrastructure to match. Mountain safety (altitude, weather) is the primary consideration, not security.

AJK (Murree, Neelum Valley)

Azad Kashmir's tourist hill stations are very safe and see enormous domestic and international tourism. Murree, Neelum Valley, and Rawalkot are standard tourist destinations with good infrastructure.

Getting Around

Transport in
Pakistan

✈️
Domestic Flights

PIA, AirSial, and SereneAir connect Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, Peshawar, Quetta, Skardu, and Gilgit. Skardu flights are spectacular. Book well ahead in summer. Fares: USD 40–120 one way.

🚂
Trains

Pakistan Railways connects Karachi to Peshawar via Lahore and Rawalpindi. AC Business Class is comfortable and scenic. The Lahore–Rawalpindi route passes Taxila. Book 48 hours ahead. Fares: USD 5–25.

🚌
Long-Distance Buses

Daewoo and Faisal Movers run premium AC coaches between major cities. Very comfortable, punctual, and affordable. Islamabad–Lahore (4 hrs, USD 10) is a reliable daily service.

🚗
Private Car + Driver

The best option for heritage touring. A driver + Toyota Corolla costs USD 60–100/day including fuel. For mountain roads (Hunza, Swat), 4WD is essential. We arrange trusted drivers for all booked tours.

Money & Payments

Pakistan is predominantly cash-based. USD and GBP exchange at all major city airports and exchange bureaus at good rates. ATMs work for Visa and Mastercard in cities.

  • ✓
    Bring USD or GBP cash as backup
  • ✓
    Exchange at banks or official bureaus only
  • !
    ATMs unreliable in remote areas — carry enough cash
  • !
    Credit cards accepted only at top hotels

Tipping Guide

Tipping is appreciated but not expected everywhere. General norms:

  • ✓
    Tour guides: PKR 500–2000 per day (USD 2–8)
  • ✓
    Drivers: PKR 200–500 per day
  • ✓
    Restaurants: 10% if service was good
  • ✓
    Hotel porters: PKR 100–200 per bag

What to Pack

The Pakistan
Packing List

Clothing Essentials

  • Loose, lightweight trousers (not shorts in public)
  • Long-sleeved shirts / tops for site visits
  • Dupatta / headscarf (women — essential for shrines)
  • Comfortable walking shoes with good grip
  • Sandals for indoor spaces and shrine visits
  • Fleece or light jacket (evenings cool, north is cold)
  • Sun hat or cap for outdoor sites
  • Sunglasses (strong UV, especially in GB)

Health & Hygiene

  • Prescription medications with documentation
  • Imodium / diarrhoea relief (essential)
  • Oral rehydration sachets
  • High-factor sunscreen (SPF50+)
  • Insect repellent (DEET-based)
  • Hand sanitiser — carry at all times
  • Water purification tablets as backup
  • Altitude sickness tablets (for GB — acetazolamide)

Documents & Tech

  • Passport + 2 photocopies stored separately
  • Visa documents — print and digital copies
  • Travel insurance details and 24hr helpline
  • Unlocked phone — buy a local SIM at airport
  • Power bank — sites don't have charging points
  • Multi-plug adapter (Type C/D/G used in Pakistan)
  • Camera + extra batteries (no power on-site)
  • Offline maps downloaded (Maps.me works in remote areas)

Health Preparation

Staying Healthy
in Pakistan

💉
Recommended Vaccinations

Consult your doctor 6–8 weeks before travel. Routine vaccines should be current. Recommended additions: Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid, Rabies (if going to rural areas), Japanese Encephalitis (if going into rural KPK in season).

🚰
Water Safety

Never drink tap water in Pakistan. Bottled water is cheap and available everywhere (PKR 30–60 per litre). In mountain areas and villages, filtered water from reputable sources is acceptable. Carry rehydration sachets.

🏔️
Altitude Sickness

Gilgit-Baltistan reaches 3,000–5,000m altitude. Acclimatise properly — don't fly to Skardu (2,200m) and immediately start hiking. Diamox (acetazolamide) prescribed before travel significantly reduces risk.

🏥
Medical Facilities

Islamabad and Lahore have good private hospitals. Karachi has excellent facilities. Agha Khan University Hospital in Karachi is internationally accredited. In rural areas and mountains, facilities are very limited — carry comprehensive medical kit.

☀️
Heat & Sun

Pakistani summers are extreme — Punjab and Sindh reach 45°C+ in June–August. Outdoor site visits should be 06:00–10:00 and 16:00–18:00 only. Wear loose white clothing, drink 3+ litres daily, and carry electrolytes.

🦟
Malaria & Dengue

Malaria risk exists in rural Balochistan, parts of Sindh, and KPK especially in monsoon season. Dengue fever is present in urban areas in summer. Use DEET-based repellent consistently, sleep under nets in rural areas.
