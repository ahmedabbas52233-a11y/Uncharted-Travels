Our Story

How We
Began

Uncharted Travels was founded in 2010 by a group of Lahore-based historians, archaeologists, and travel professionals who shared a single frustration: Pakistan's remarkable heritage was invisible to the world.

The country held six UNESCO World Heritage Sites, one of the world's oldest urban civilisations, the finest Mughal architecture outside the Taj Mahal, and a Buddhist heritage of staggering depth — yet international tourism to historical sites was negligible. Most visitors who came were trekkers heading for the mountains, unaware that the ruins beneath them were equally extraordinary.

We began with a single Taxila day tour for a group of foreign diplomats based in Islamabad. Word spread. Within two years we were operating full heritage circuits across Punjab, KPK, and Sindh. Today we are Pakistan's most comprehensive heritage travel company — still based in Islamabad, still led by people who have spent their lives studying these landscapes.

What We Stand For

Our Four
Commitments

01

Scholarly Depth

Every guide on every tour has specialist knowledge of the site they are presenting. We work with archaeologists, historians, and published researchers. We never repeat myths. We tell you what the evidence says.

02

Honest Safety Guidance

We do not minimise risks to sell tours, nor exaggerate them to justify premium prices. We give you an accurate picture of each region's conditions and only operate where we are genuinely confident in your safety.

03

Local Partnership

Over 80% of what you spend with us stays in Pakistan — with local drivers, local restaurants, locally-owned guesthouses, and local artisans. We actively avoid the international hotel chains that extract value from communities.

04

Heritage Preservation

We contribute 5% of all heritage tour profits to the Aga Khan Cultural Service Pakistan and the Taxila Institute of Asian Civilizations — both working to preserve the sites we depend on.

Our Journey

Milestones

2010

First Multi-Province Circuit
Launched the Punjab–Sindh Heritage Circuit — the first tour to combine Taxila, Lahore, Mohenjo-daro and Makli in one itinerary.

Became the first tour operator to run a programme visiting all six of Pakistan's UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Sold out within three weeks of launch.
