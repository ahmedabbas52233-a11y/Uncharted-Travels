Taxila

Pakistan's Greatest Archaeological Landscape · 600 BCE – 500 CE

Taxila is not one site — it is a 30 square kilometre archaeological landscape containing the ruins of three successive cities and at least twelve major Buddhist monasteries, stupas, and temple complexes. It was the most important centre of Buddhist scholarship in the ancient world.

Alexander the Great arrived in 326 BCE. The Indo-Greeks, Kushans, and Guptas all built here. Taxila was the Oxford of ancient Asia — Chanakya, the author of the Arthashastra, studied and taught here.

Mohenjo-daro

The World's First Planned City · 2500–1900 BCE

Built in 2500 BCE, Mohenjo-daro was the world's first planned city — with straight streets, brick houses, a sophisticated sewage system, and the world's earliest known public swimming pool. At its peak it housed 40,000 people.

The Indus Valley Civilisation it anchored covered 1.25 million square kilometres — larger than Mesopotamia and Egypt combined, yet it remains the least-known of the world's founding civilisations.

Lahore Fort

The Mughal Empire's Greatest Fortress · 1566 CE

Lahore Fort — Shahi Qila — was the seat of Mughal power for over two centuries. Every great Mughal emperor from Akbar to Aurangzeb built here. Its 21 monuments include the Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors), Diwan-e-Aam, and the extraordinary tiled Picture Wall — 140m of the finest faience work in Asia.

Period1566 CE – ongoing (active heritage)
UNESCO Listed1981 · Fort & Shalamar Gardens
LocationWalled City, Lahore

Takht-i-Bahi

South Asia's Best-Preserved Buddhist Monastery · 1st–7th CE

Takht-i-Bahi — Throne of Origins — is the best-preserved Buddhist monastery in South Asia. Perched on a 152m hilltop in KPK, it was continuously occupied for over 500 years and escaped the destruction that levelled most Gandhara sites. The stupa courts, meditation cells, and assembly halls remain largely intact.

Period1st century BCE – 7th century CE
UNESCO Listed1980 · Buddhist Ruins
LocationMardan District, KPK
