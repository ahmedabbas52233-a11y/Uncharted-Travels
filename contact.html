The short answer is yes — for the heritage regions we cover. Lahore, Islamabad, Taxila, Hunza and the major Sindh heritage sites are all safe for tourists. We monitor conditions continuously and will never operate in areas we consider unsafe. Our full safety guide gives a detailed breakdown by region.

We assist with Pakistan's e-Visa application process and all NOC permits required for specific areas — at no extra charge. The e-Visa itself must be applied for in your name, but we guide you through the process step by step and can prepare supporting documentation.

Absolutely. Our open group tours run with 6–12 participants and are a wonderful way to meet fellow heritage enthusiasts. Solo travellers are very welcome — we also offer private tours for those who prefer a bespoke pace and itinerary.

Full refund if cancelled 30+ days before departure. 50% refund for cancellations 15–29 days before. No refund within 14 days — but we always try to offer alternative dates. We strongly recommend travel insurance covering cancellation from the moment you book.

Most heritage tours involve moderate walking on uneven ground — we recommend reasonable mobility but no specific fitness level is required. Some sites (Takht-i-Bahi, Rohtas Fort) involve steeper terrain. We tailor pacing to every group and can arrange vehicle support for sites where distance is a concern.

All tours include: expert guide fees, ground transport, accommodation as specified, breakfast daily, entry fees to all listed sites, and all NOC permits. International flights, personal travel insurance, and visa fees are not included. A detailed cost breakdown is provided before you confirm any booking.
