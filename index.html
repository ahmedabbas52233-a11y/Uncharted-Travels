Our Mission

The World's Most
Underrated
Heritage Nation

Pakistan is home to six UNESCO World Heritage Sites, one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, and the ruins of a civilisation that preceded Egypt's pyramids by five centuries. Yet it remains largely unexplored by international travellers.

At Uncharted Travels, we've spent over a decade building the most comprehensive land travel guides and expert-led tour programmes in the country — covering every province, every era, and every road worth taking.

We go beyond Taxila. From the sun-baked ruins of Mohenjo-daro in Sindh to the Kalash valleys of Chitral, the Mughal grandeur of Lahore to the fortress deserts of Balochistan — this is Pakistan in full.

Iconic Land Routes

Roads That
Changed History

Full Travel Guide
01

The Grand Trunk Road

Peshawar → Rawalpindi → Lahore · The ancient Mughal highway used by emperors and armies for 2,500 years. Pakistan's most historically saturated corridor.

UNESCO Corridor
1,600 km · 3–7 days
02

Karakoram Highway

Rawalpindi → Gilgit → Khunjerab Pass · The world's highest international paved road, following the ancient Silk Road into China through some of Earth's most dramatic scenery.

Silk Road
1,300 km · 4–10 days
03

Makran Coastal Highway

Karachi → Gwadar · Along the ancient Arabian Sea trade routes, past Princess of Hope, moonscapes, and fishing villages unchanged for centuries.

Coastal Route
700 km · 2–3 days
04

Gandhara Buddhist Trail

Taxila → Peshawar → Swat → Takht-i-Bahi · Following the footsteps of Buddhist monks and Gandharan sculptors across three UNESCO sites.

Heritage Trail
450 km · 3–5 days
05

Cholistan Desert Circuit

Bahawalpur → Derawar Fort → Cholistan · The forgotten Rajputana desert with a 30-metre medieval fort rising from golden dunes — virtually unknown to visitors.

Desert Adventure
300 km · 2 days
